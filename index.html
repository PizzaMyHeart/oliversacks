Oliver Sacks

The poet laureate of medicine

Oliver Sacks was a neurologist and best-selling author who achieved a level of acclaim and renown uncommon among scientists. His case studies, written with a strong emphasis on the human narrative often overlooked in the face of weird and sometimes disturbing neurological diseases, have been published in books and in the New Yorker and the New York Review of Books. Through his written works, he introduced disorders such as Tourette's and Asperger's to a wider audience.
